Social Anxiety Disorder

Social anxiety disorder, also known as social phobia, is a psychological condition characterized by an intense and persistent fear of social situations. People with social anxiety disorder experience overwhelming anxiety and self-consciousness when faced with social interactions, leading to a strong desire to avoid or escape from such situations. This condition can significantly impact an individual's daily life, relationships, and overall well-being.

Generalized Social Anxiety Disorder (GSAD)

In GSAD, individuals experience anxiety and fear in a wide range of social situations, not limited to specific settings or circumstances. They may feel uncomfortable and anxious in various social interactions, such as speaking in public, attending parties, meeting new people, or engaging in small talk.

Performance Social Anxiety Disorder

This subtype of SAD is centered around performance-related situations. Individuals with performance SAD may experience intense anxiety and fear when they are required to perform or be observed, such as during public speaking, giving a presentation, or playing a musical instrument in front of others.

Our Services

Our primary goal is to raise awareness about social anxiety disorder and combat the stigma surrounding mental health. We strive to promote understanding and empathy among individuals, families, and communities. Through education and knowledge sharing, we aim to empower those with social anxiety disorder to seek help, access appropriate resources, and find effective strategies for managing their symptoms.

About Us

Members can join discussions, share their thoughts, and provide encouragement to one another, fostering a sense of camaraderie and understanding.
